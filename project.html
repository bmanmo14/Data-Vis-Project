World Leader Religious Affiliations and Their Sociopolitical Effects

According to Pew Research, in 2017, around 84% of people across the world claimed a religion. A region’s predominant religious affiliations can affect the political, sociological, and environmental aspects of the region in profound ways. The goal of this tool is to visualize these effects. Click the help button to learn how it works!

For the most part, the religious inclinations of any particular region stay consistent over time—and when they do change, it’s usually gradual. The goal of this visualization is not just to provide a gradual view of these societal effects, but a year-by-year comparison. Since regional religious inclinations usually shift slowly, the religion of the leading political figure is used to represent a region's religion rather than the predominant religion of the region. We realize this is not representative of the region as a whole, but political figures have a profound effect on the organizational changes that occur in a society. Thus, we hope that some interesting patterns will arise.

We acknowledge that it would be irresponsible to assume causality from this tool.
